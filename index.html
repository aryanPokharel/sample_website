Nepal

The Land Of Buddha and Mount Everest

The Federal Democratic Republic nation of Nepal is located in the Himalayas, tucked in a geopolitically significant region between China and India.

Introduction To Nepal

THE LAND OF THE BRAVE GORKHAS!

Nepal, country of Asia, lying along the southern slopes of the Himalayan mountain ranges. It is a landlocked country located between India to the east, south, and west and the Tibet Autonomous Region of China to the north. Its territory extends roughly 500 miles (800 kilometres) from east to west and 90 to 150 miles from north to south. The capital is Kathmandu. Nepal, long under the rule of hereditary prime ministers favouring a policy of isolation, remained closed to the outside world until a palace revolt in 1950 restored the crown’s authority in 1951; the country gained admission to the United Nations in 1955. In 1991 the kingdom established a multiparty parliamentary system. In 2008, however, after a decadelong period of violence and turbulent negotiation with a strong Maoist insurgency, the monarchy was dissolved, and Nepal was declared a democratic republic.

Important Destinations

Gorkha

Gorkha is the genesis of the modern day Nepal. King Prithivi Narayan Shah, in 1799 BS. started the unification campaign of Nepal from his small hill state of Gorkha. This led to the foundation of Kingdom of Nepal by 1867 BS. The present day Gorkha district is home to the historic palace.

Kalapani

Kalapani is a very strategic location of Nepal. Located towards the north-western most part of the country, this place is home to eye candy natural beauty along with culturally rich settlements of people. The lipulekh pass located in the region is significant to Nepal for tri-national trade between Nepal, China and India.

Pokhara

Pokhara is Nepal's number 1 adventure and leisure destination, a gateway to treks in the Annapurna region with plenty of entertainment for individual travellers and their families. In Pokhara you can experience the excitement of adventure: boating, hiking, pony rides, paragliding, bungee, zipline or simply relaxing at one of the several lakes in the valley. Relax at the shores of Fewa lake in Pokhara Pokhara will overwhelm you with its natural beauty and great photo ops.

Illam

A brisk walk in a tea garden, picnic in the peaceful setting and sightseeing stops, short hikes along gentle hills, or explore the nearby woods – enjoy all this and more in the greenery of Ilam. An excellent getaway from city life, Ilam is famous for its tea. It is a district largely covered in tea bushes with a hill town right beside a plantation enjoying views of pristine landscapes made up of sloping tea gardens.

Lumbini

Known throughout the world as the birthplace of Gautam Buddha, this place marks the genesis of Buddhism. The 'Maya Devi Temple' marks the exact birthplace i.e.accurate birthspot of Gautam Buddha, The Light Of Asia. Thousands of pilgrims and tourists flock towards Lumbini to see the birthplace of Buddha as well as to immerse themselves in the peaceful tranqulity of various pagodas and meditation halls scattered near the Maya Devi Temple.

Mustang

Mustang is a breath taking mountainous tourist destination of Nepal. Being one of the driest regions of the country, Mustang receives very less or no rainfall at all throughout the year. However, Mustang holds a good reputation for being the windiest place of Nepal. The Muktinath temple located in Mustang is one of the major tourist destination which attracts pilgrims and explorers from other parts of Nepal as well as International communities.
